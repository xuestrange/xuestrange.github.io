Make Recommendations More Effective Through Framings:Impacts of User-Versus Iten-Based Framing on Recommendation Click-Thoughs

Gai, Phyliss Jia, and Anne-Kathrin Klesse. “Making Recommendations More Effective Through Framings: Impacts of User- Versus Item-Based Framings on Recommendation Click-Throughs.” Journal of Marketing 83, no. 6 (November 2019): 61–75. https://doi.org/10.1177/0022242919873901.

In a Word

The paper explores whether user-based or item-based is more effective in trigging clicks on a recommendation by 6 experiments.

Motivation

Prior researches focus on recommendation algorithms or characteristics of recommended products, no describing the recommendations.
Reasons.

  1. Many recommendation system rely on the input from both users and items, firm can choose to highlight which one.
  2. altering recommendation system costs nearly zero

Research Design

6 experiments:
compare these two framings while keeping the actual recommendation constant.

Result

  • study 1a 1b: user-based increases click-through in terms of item-based.
  • study 2:consummption experience influence recommendation result.
  • study 3 4:the effect shrinks and even reverses for unattractive focal products.
  • study 4 5:dissimilarity among consumers decrease the effect of recommendation.
